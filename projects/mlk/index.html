Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.

Civil Rights Activist, Pastor, & Noble Peace Prize Winner


Here's a timeline of Reverand King's life:
  • 1929 - Martin Luther King is born Michael Luther King in Atlanta, Georgia. He later changes his name to Martin.
  • 1944 - King began his freshman year at Morehouse College
  • 1948 - King is appointed Assistant Pastor at Ebenezer Baptist Church. He also receives his B.A. degree in sociology from Morehouse College and begins study at Crozier Theological Seminary in Chester, Pennsylvania
  • 1951 - King delivers the valedictorian address at Crozier, graduating with a bachelor of divinity degree. Later that year, King begins studying systematic theology at Boston University
  • 1953 - King and Coretta Scott get married at the Scott family home near Marion, Alabama
  • 1955 - Graduates with his Ph.D. in systematic theology. Later that year, Rosa Parks refuses to give up her seat on a bus in protest. King becomes a leader of the Montgomery Bus Boycott
  • 1956 - King's home is bombed; his wife & child are not injured. During a speech later that evening, King pleads for non-violence. Later that year, the Supreme Court rules that the Alabama bus segregation laws are unconstitutional
  • 1959 - King travels to India and meets with Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru as well as the family of Mahatma Gandhi
  • 1960 - Becomes a co-pastor alongside his father at Ebenezer Baptist Church in Atlanta. King is also arrested during a sit-in demonstration at a restaurant in Greensboro, NC
  • 1963 - King writes the famous "Letter from Birmingham Jail" after being arrested again. Conflict in Birmingham reaches a critical level when police use high-pressure fire hoses against demonstrators.
  • 1963 - King delivers the famous "I Have a Dream" speech during the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom
  • 1964 - King's book "Why Can't We Wait" is published. Later, Martin Luther King Jr. becomes the youngest person to receive the Nobel Peace Prize in Oslo, Norway
  • 1966 - King starts to focus on other social issues, beginning with the "March Against Fear" from Memphis to Jackson
  • 1967 - King delivers the "Beyond Vietnam" speech at Riverside Church in New York City, demanding that the U.S. end the war. King publishes the book "Where Do We Go From Here: Chaos or Community?"
  • 1968 - King delivers his final "I've Been to the Mountaintop" speech
  • 1968 - Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. is assassinated on the balcony of the Lorrain Motel in Memphis, Tennessee. He is buried in Atlanta.
